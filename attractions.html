Places to Discover

Around Kotagiri

The Nilgiris, Unhurried

Kotagiri sits at 1,793 metres above sea level, surrounded by waterfalls, ancient forests, sweeping viewpoints, and tea estates that stretch to the horizon. Our resort is your ideal base for exploring it all — at your own pace.

Catherine Falls — cascading waterfall in lush green Nilgiri forest
I

Natural Wonder

Catherine Falls

One of the most breathtaking waterfalls in the Nilgiris, Catherine Falls cascades down from a height of approximately 250 feet through dense shola forest. The drive itself is a winding journey through misty hills and tea estates.

Best visited in the early morning when the mist still clings to the canopy and the falls are in full roar. A short walk through the forest leads to the viewpoint, where the sight rewards every step.

Kodanadu View — panoramic viewpoint over Nilgiri valleys and plains
II

Scenic Viewpoint

Kodanadu View

Perched at the edge of the Nilgiris, Kodanadu Viewpoint offers a dramatic panorama of the valleys below and the distant plains stretching to the horizon. On clear days, the views extend for miles in every direction.

Sunrise visits are legendary — the valley fills with a sea of clouds while the peaks catch the first gold of morning. A truly unmissable Nilgiri experience, just a short drive from the resort.

Longwood Forest — shola forest trail with ancient trees and ferns
III

Ancient Forest

Longwood Forest

Longwood Shola is one of the finest examples of tropical montane forest in the Nilgiris — a cathedral of ancient trees, moss-covered boulders, and fern-lined trails that feel untouched by time.

A paradise for birdwatchers and nature lovers alike. The forest is home to an extraordinary variety of endemic species, and the silence within is profound — broken only by birdsong and the wind through the canopy.
